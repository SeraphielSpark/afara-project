Souls of the South & North

African Americans

From the cotton fields of Mississippi to the brownstones of Harlem, from the migrations to Chicago to the protests in Selma a people who built a country and were never paid for it.

African Americans carry a particular gravity in the Diaspora. Most cannot trace a specific village. Most cannot speak the tongue their great-great-grandparents dreamed in. Yet from this very deprivation came one of the most influential cultures the modern world has ever produced: gospel, blues, jazz, hip-hop, civil rights, Black church theology, the literature of Toni Morrison and James Baldwin, the leadership of Du Bois, King and Malcolm. We honour the genius forged in chains, and we walk together with our American kin in the long unfinished work of liberation.

Islands of the Diaspora

Afro-Caribbeans

Jamaica, Haiti, Trinidad, Barbados, Cuba, the Dominican Republic, Guyana a sea of small nations whose cultural reach spans the planet.

The Caribbean is where the Diaspora first improvised itself into existence. Stolen Africans of dozens of ethnicities Igbo, Yoruba, Akan, Kongo, Mandinka were thrown together on plantations, and from that violent collision came something new and astonishing: Vodou and Santería, reggae and calypso, soca and dancehall, the first free Black republic on earth. Caribbean people taught the Diaspora how to keep dancing under empire. They are our memory keepers and our rhythm.

Latin Roots, African Soul

Afro-Latinos

Brazil, Colombia, Venezuela, Mexico, Peru, the Dominican Republic Latin America is, by population, the largest Diaspora region on earth.

More Africans were brought to Brazil alone than to all of North America combined. Salvador da Bahia is, in many ways, the most African city outside of Africa. From Candomblé to capoeira, from samba to cumbia, from the Quilombo dos Palmares to the Garífuna of Honduras Afro-Latinos hold an enormous, often invisible, share of the Diaspora story. We are working to make them visible to themselves, to their nations, and to the world.

Diaspora in the Old World

Africans in Europe

London, Paris, Lisbon, Amsterdam, Berlin, Rome children and grandchildren of empire, building Black Britain, Black France, Black Germany.

Europe's African Diaspora is in many ways the youngest and the most rapidly growing. Some are the descendants of post-war Caribbean migrations to the United Kingdom and France. Others arrived from Senegal, Ghana, Nigeria, Somalia, Angola and Cape Verde in the 1970s, 80s and 90s. They are remaking the cultures of their host cities but they often live with a particular question: am I European, African, both, neither? Wisdom of Afara was born in this very community, and we are committed to giving European Africans the cultural anchor empire has tried to deny them.

The Continent that Birthed Us All

Mother Africa

54 nations. Over 2,000 languages. The cradle of humanity itself. The mother who never forgot.

No Diaspora project is honest without the continent at its centre. Africa is not a backdrop, not an idea, not a memory it is a living, sovereign, breathtakingly diverse home of 1.4 billion people, with the world's youngest population and its oldest civilisations. From the libraries of Timbuktu to the mountains of Ethiopia, from the Yoruba kingdoms to the Bantu migrations, from Lagos studios to Kigali tech startups Africa is the present and the future, not only the past. We honour those who never left, who held the fire while we were taken, and we listen to them as elders.
